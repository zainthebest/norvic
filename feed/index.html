]]> If you’ve been dreaming of living in Spain without the hassle of working locally, the Spain Non Lucrative Visa might be exactly what you need. But when can you apply? Who qualifies? And where do you start the process? This comprehensive guide will answer those questions and more, helping you understand the key requirements and steps for obtaining this popular visa in 2025.

What Is the Spain Non Lucrative Visa?

The Non-Lucrative Visa is a residence permit for non-EU nationals who want to live in Spain long-term but do not intend to engage in any work or professional activity within the country. It’s ideal for retirees, remote workers with clients outside Spain, or anyone with sufficient passive income who wants to enjoy Spain’s lifestyle without needing a work contract or local employment.

When Can You Apply for the Spain Non Lucrative Visa?

You must apply for the Non-Lucrative Visa from your country of legal residence before traveling to Spain. The application is submitted through the nearest Spanish consulate or embassy. It’s advisable to start the process well in advance, as visa processing times can vary but generally take several weeks to a few months.

Once your visa is approved, you must enter Spain within 90 days of the visa issuance date. After arriving, you have 30 days to apply for your Foreigner Identification Card (TIE), which formalizes your residency status.

Who Is Eligible for the Spain Non Lucrative Visa?

To qualify, applicants must meet several key criteria:

  • Non-EU/EEA/Swiss nationals who want to reside in Spain without working locally.
  • Proof of sufficient financial means to support themselves and any dependents without employment in Spain.
  • A clean criminal record from your country of residence and any country where you have lived in the past five years.
  • Valid private health insurance covering all risks insured by Spain’s public health system, with no co-payments or deductibles.
  • A medical certificate confirming you are free of contagious diseases as per WHO regulations.

How Much Money Do You Need?

Financial requirements are one of the most important parts of the application. For 2025, the Spanish government bases the income threshold on the IPREM (Public Multiple Effects Income Indicator), which is approximately €600 per month.

  • The main applicant must show at least 400% of the IPREM, which equals roughly €2,400 per month or €28,800 per year.
  • For each additional family member, an extra 100% of the IPREM is required, about €600 per month or €7,200 per year per dependent.

Acceptable proof of income includes:

  • Bank statements from the last 3 to 6 months.
  • Pension income.
  • Investment earnings or dividends.
  • Rental income.
  • Savings or certificates of deposit.

It’s important that these funds are stable and guaranteed for the duration of your stay. The financial threshold is reassessed annually, so applicants must confirm they meet the updated requirements at the time of application and renewal.

What Documents Are Required?

The application package typically includes:

  • Completed visa application forms.
  • Recent passport-size photographs.
  • Valid passport with at least one year validity and two blank pages.
  • Proof of financial means as described above.
  • Private health insurance certificate.
  • Criminal record certificate issued within the last 3 months.
  • Medical certificate.
  • For family members: marriage certificate, birth certificates, and proof of financial dependence if applicable.

Where Do You Apply?

You must apply at the Spanish consulate or embassy in your country of legal residence. Each consulate may have slight variations in document requirements or processing times, so it’s crucial to check their specific instructions before submitting your application.

After approval, you will collect your visa from the consulate and then travel to Spain to complete the residency process by obtaining your Foreigner Identification Card (TIE) at the local Foreigners’ Office.

What Are the Benefits of the Non-Lucrative Visa?

  • Long-term residency in Spain without the need to work locally.
  • Ability to bring immediate family members (spouse, dependent children, and dependent relatives) under the same visa, provided you can prove additional financial means.
  • Freedom to work remotely for clients or employers outside Spain, making it attractive for digital nomads.
  • Access to Spain’s healthcare system through private insurance.
  • Pathway to renewal and eventual permanent residency or citizenship after meeting residency requirements.

What Are the Limitations?

  • You cannot engage in any work or professional activity within Spain.
  • The visa is initially issued for one year and must be renewed annually, requiring proof that you still meet financial and insurance requirements.
  • You must maintain continuous residence in Spain, with some limits on time spent outside the country.

Final Thoughts

The Spain Non-Lucrative Visa offers an excellent opportunity for those seeking to enjoy Spain’s rich culture, pleasant climate, and high quality of life without the need to work locally. However, meeting the financial and documentation requirements is crucial for a successful application.

Overview of the Grenada Citizenship by Investment Program

The Grenada CBI program allows investors and their families to obtain citizenship by making a qualifying investment in the country. Applicants can choose between two main routes: a non-refundable contribution to the National Transformation Fund (NTF) or a real estate investment in government-approved projects.

The minimum investment for the NTF donation starts at $235,000 for a single applicant or a family of up to four members. For the real estate option, the minimum investment is $270,000, plus an additional non-refundable government fee. Real estate investments must be held for at least five years to qualify.

One of the key advantages of Grenada’s program is its relatively fast processing time, typically between three to six months, allowing investors to obtain citizenship and a Grenadian passport promptly.

Benefits of Grenadian Citizenship

Grenadian citizenship offers extensive benefits that appeal to global investors:

This visa enables investors to live and work in the United States by investing in a qualifying business, providing a valuable gateway to the US market.
  • No Residency Requirement: The program does not require applicants to reside in Grenada before or after citizenship is granted, offering flexibility for those who wish to maintain their current lifestyle.
  • Family Inclusion: The main applicant can include their spouse, children under 30, unmarried siblings over 18, and dependent parents and grandparents, ensuring the entire family benefits from citizenship.
  • Dual Citizenship Allowed: Grenada permits dual citizenship, so investors do not have to renounce their original nationality.

The Investment Routes Explained

1. National Transformation Fund Contribution:
This is a straightforward donation to a government fund that supports national development projects such as infrastructure, education, and healthcare. It is a non-refundable payment but offers a quick and hassle-free path to citizenship.

2. Real Estate Investment:
Investors can purchase property in approved luxury resorts or developments. This option not only grants citizenship but also offers the potential for rental income and capital appreciation. The property must be held for five years, but after that period, it can be sold freely.

Due Diligence and Application Process

Grenada maintains strict due diligence standards to protect the integrity of its citizenship program. Applicants undergo thorough background checks, including criminal record verification and financial source assessments. The process ensures that only reputable individuals are granted citizenship.

The application process involves submitting detailed documentation, including passports, proof of investment funds, police clearances, and medical certificates. The D2 Visa: Empowering Entrepreneurs and Business Owners

The D2 Visa is tailored for non-EU nationals who intend to start or invest in a business in Portugal. Since its introduction, it has become a preferred route for entrepreneurs looking to establish a presence in the European market.

Who should consider the D2 Visa?

  • Entrepreneurs with innovative business concepts.
  • Established business owners seeking expansion into Portugal.
  • Independent professionals offering services within Portugal.

Key requirements include:

  • A comprehensive business plan demonstrating economic and social benefits to Portugal.
  • Obtaining a Portuguese Tax Identification Number (NIF) and opening a local bank account.
  • Proof of accommodation in Portugal.
  • Evidence of sufficient financial resources to support both personal living expenses and business operations.
  • Valid travel medical insurance covering at least six months.

The initial visa allows entry into Portugal for four months, during which applicants apply for a residence permit. This permit is valid for two years and renewable for three years thereafter. After five years of legal residence, applicants may apply for Portuguese citizenship.

The D7 Visa: Ideal for Retirees and Passive Income Earners

The D7 Visa is designed for individuals who have a stable passive income and wish to reside in Portugal without engaging in business activities. This visa is particularly popular among retirees, freelancers, and those with rental or investment income.

Who fits the D7 Visa profile?

  • Retirees with pensions or other steady income streams.
  • Freelancers or remote workers with consistent passive income.
  • Individuals with sufficient legal income to sustain themselves in Portugal.

Essential requirements include:

  • Proof of regular passive income meeting or exceeding the minimum threshold, typically aligned with Portugal’s minimum wage.
  • Accommodation evidence in Portugal.
  • Valid health insurance.
  • Clean criminal record.

Like the D2 Visa, the D7 Visa leads to a residence permit with the same validity and renewal terms, and eligibility for citizenship after five years.

The D8 Visa: A New Frontier for Digital Nomads

Recognizing the global rise of remote work, Portugal introduced the D8 Visa to attract digital nomads and remote professionals who work for foreign companies or clients while residing in Portugal.

Who should apply for the D8 Visa?

  • Remote employees working for companies outside Portugal.
  • Freelancers providing digital services internationally.

Key criteria include:

  • Proof of remote employment or freelance contracts.
  • A minimum monthly income generally set at four times the Portuguese minimum wage (approximately €3,480).
  • Proof of accommodation and valid health insurance.

The D8 Visa grants a residence permit similar to the D2 and D7, offering a pathway to permanent residency and citizenship.

Whether you are looking for visa-free access to over 140 countries, including the Schengen Area and China, or a tax-friendly jurisdiction, Grenada CBI program provides a fast, reliable, and family-friendly route to citizenship.

Why Choose Grenada Citizenship by Investment?

Grenada’s CBI program stands out for several reasons:

  • Visa-Free Travel: Grenadian passport holders enjoy visa-free or visa-on-arrival access to 148 countries, including the UK, EU Schengen countries, China, Russia, and more.
  • No Residency Requirement: You are not required to live in or visit Grenada before or after obtaining citizenship.
  • Family Inclusion: The program allows you to include your spouse, dependent children under 30, parents or grandparents over 55, and unmarried siblings under 18.
  • US E-2 Visa Eligibility: Grenadian citizens can apply for the US E-2 investor visa, allowing them to live and work in the United States.
  • Tax Advantages: Grenada imposes no worldwide income, inheritance, or capital gains taxes.
  • Fast Processing: Citizenship can be granted within approximately six months.
  • Dual Citizenship Permitted: You can keep your current nationality while enjoying the benefits of Grenadian citizenship.

Investment Options

There are two main investment routes to qualify for Grenada citizenship:

1. National Transformation Fund (NTF) Contribution

This is a non-refundable donation to Grenada’s government fund supporting national development projects. The minimum contribution is:

  • $235,000 for a single applicant
  • $250,000 for a family of four

Additional dependents require extra contributions. This route is popular for its simplicity and quick processing.

2. Real Estate Investment

Alternatively, applicants can invest in government-approved real estate projects with a minimum investment of $270,000. This option requires holding the property for at least five years. It is suitable for those interested in property ownership in Grenada alongside citizenship.

Additional Fees

Applicants should also budget for due diligence fees (around $5,000 per applicant), processing fees, and passport issuance fees. Proof of legitimate source of funds and financial standing is mandatory.

Eligibility Criteria

To apply for Grenadian citizenship by investment, you must:

  • Be at least 18 years old.
  • Have a clean criminal record, verified through police clearance certificates from all countries where you have lived for more than six months in the past decade.
  • Provide proof of legal source of funds.
  • Pass a thorough due diligence process.
  • Complete a mandatory virtual citizenship interview.
  • Submit all required documents, including valid passports, birth certificates, medical certificates, and financial statements.

Certain nationalities may face additional scrutiny or restrictions.

Step-by-Step Application Process

Step 1: Choose Your Investment Route

Decide whether to make a non-refundable contribution to the National Transformation Fund or invest in government-approved real estate.

Step 2: Select an Authorized Agent

Grenada’s Citizenship by Investment Unit (CIU) requires applications to be submitted through government-approved agents. Choose a licensed agent or law firm, such as Forecast Immigration Law Firm, to guide you through the process.

Step 3: Prepare and Submit Documentation

Gather all necessary documents, including proof of identity, financial records, police clearances, and medical certificates. Your agent will help format and submit your application to the Grenada Investment Migration Agency (IMA).

Step 4: Due Diligence and Interview

The IMA conducts rigorous background checks and due diligence. Applicants aged 17 and above must complete a virtual interview to verify their suitability.

Step 5: Receive Approval in Principle

Within approximately 60 to 90 days, you will receive an “Approved in Principle” letter if your application passes due diligence.

Step 6: Make Your Investment

Complete the investment by transferring funds to the National Transformation Fund or purchasing real estate. Provide proof of payment to the CIU.

Step 7: Take the Oath of Allegiance and Obtain Citizenship

Upon confirmation, you and your family will take the oath of allegiance at a Grenadian embassy, consulate, or authorized location. You will then receive your citizenship certificate and passport.

Timeline and Processing

The entire process typically takes around six months but can be faster depending on document readiness and due diligence outcomes. Grenada has recently implemented an automated citizenship certificate system that streamlines approvals and reduces processing times.

Life as a Grenadian Citizen

Grenada offers a peaceful, tropical lifestyle with beautiful beaches, a warm climate, and a welcoming community. As a citizen, you have the right to live, work, and study in Grenada and enjoy the benefits of membership in the Organization of Eastern Caribbean States (OECS), which includes residency privileges in member countries.

The Grenadian passport is valid for five years and can be renewed indefinitely. There is no requirement to reside in Grenada to maintain citizenship, making it a flexible option for global citizens.
